Biography

About Me

Scientist, builder, writer, leader — here is who I am and what drives me.

I am Vicent Kamya, a PhD chemist specialising in flow chemistry and laboratory automation. My research sits at the boundary between synthetic organic chemistry, continuous manufacturing, and medicinal chemistry. I am driven by a conviction that science done well — and shared widely — can transform communities.

My current research focuses on building automated mobile flow chemistry platforms that can produce pharmaceutical ingredients continuously and efficiently. A central project involves the continuous-flow synthesis of artemisinin, an antimalarial compound critical to global health.

Personal Mission

To harness chemistry, automation, and entrepreneurship to solve real problems in drug access, science education, and sustainable development — especially across Africa.

🔬 What I Work On

Building automated mobile flow chemistry platforms for pharmaceutical synthesis. Continuous-flow artemisinin production. Artemisinin-derived anticancer scaffolds. Natural product-based drug discovery for neglected diseases.

💡 What Drives Me

The gap between what chemistry can do and what reaches patients — especially in Africa. I believe small, automated, modular synthesis platforms could decentralise pharmaceutical production and make medicines more accessible.

✝️ What Sustains Me

Faith, family, and a genuine love of ideas. I am an active Christian leader who believes that intellectual rigour and deep faith are not enemies — and that science practised with integrity is itself an act of service.

🌍 Long-Term Vision

To build research capacity and technology infrastructure enabling African scientists and entrepreneurs to lead in pharmaceutical manufacturing, drug discovery, and science education. Institutions that combine rigorous science with entrepreneurial energy and community impact.
